Why Create Web Components?

Answer the following:

  1. Who will be using them?
  2. What framework are they using?
  3. When will they switch frameworks?
When building a component library, the technology you build them with will have advantages and disadvantages. Before choosing your technology, it is important to answer some questions.

First, you need to know who will be using your component library. How many people, teams, or projects will use your components?

Second, you need to know what frameworks your consumers are using. If you are lucky, all your consumers are using the same front-end framework, so building your components in that framework might be the best choice.

However, will they switch frameworks? We all know how fast front-end web development changes. Even if your answer to the previous question was a single framework, will they stay on that framework long enough to use your component library?

If you build your library as web components, you are choosing the browser spec and the native API over the features of a singular framework. In doing so, you are guaranteed that the components you are building will function in the browser without relying on an external library or framework.
